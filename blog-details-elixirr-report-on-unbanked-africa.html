Elixirr: Report on Unbanked Africa

According to Elixirr, ⅔ of people in Sub-Saharan Africa do not have access to a bank account.

The reasons for this are complex; political instability, a lacking infrastructure, and low levels

of confidence in existing financial institutions. This is particularly true for entrepreneurs in the

private sector whom, according to Elixirr’s field research were reluctant to use ATMs and

online banking platforms for fear of their money not reaching the intended recipient. When

entrepreneurs and potential job-creators are stifled in how they can access capital and move

it around, this creates a situation in which the economy as a whole remains stagnant.



Yet, there are over 650 million internet users which is more than there are in Europe and the

US! The use of money applications has been growing every year in large part due to a wider

availability of phones. as these applications are still using their respective country’s

increasingly inflated currencies. In other words, there needs to be a new platform that:



-Allows Africans to buy, sell and trade money securely.

-Is as convenient as using one’s existing smart phone or computer.

-Does not subject people to the issues plaguing existing currencies.



All of this is pointing towards one direction: Dnar. We are developing a new infrastructure in

which alternate currencies such as bitcoin will be available for purchase, sale and trading in a

way that is convenient and allows people to bypass the inflationary problems of current money

Advanced encryption methods will ensure that all cryptocurrencies will be stored in a trust-less

way in which private keys will be inaccessible to anyone but the user.



In advancing Africa’s economic and financial place in the world through the use of emerging

cryptocurrency technology, Africa can only go far with the use Dnar.
